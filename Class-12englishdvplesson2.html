B. Answer the following questions.

a. What type of family is thought to be the oldest form of the family?
The nuclear family is thought to be the oldest form of the family.
b. How does a family provide security to its members?
A family provides emotional and psychological security through warmth, love, and companionship. Families also provide physical security for members who are too young or too old to care for themselves.
c. What were the features of the medieval European family?
The main feature of the medieval European family was that it was a patriarchal (male-dominated) and extended family.
d. What caused the dissolution of extended families in the West?
Industrialization and urbanization led to the dissolution of extended families in the West, as many people, particularly unmarried youths, left farms to become industrial workers in urban centers.
e. What change occurred in gender roles in the modern family that emerged after the Industrial Revolution?
Following the Industrial Revolution, patriarchal rule began to give way to greater equality between the sexes. Family roles once considered exclusively male or female broke down, some couples chose not to marry legally, and rising levels of divorce led to an increase in one-parent households.
f. What is family law?
Family law is a practice area concerned with legal issues involving family relationships, such as adoption, divorce, and child custody. Attorneys practicing family law typically handle these and other related legal matters.
g. How is modern marriage defined?
Modern marriage is defined as a voluntary union, usually between a man and a woman.
h. What do special family courts try to do?
Special family courts try to deal more fairly with sensitive issues such as the custody of children.
i. What does the legislation on child labor and child abuse declare?
The legislation on child labor and child abuse declares that society has a responsibility for a child's best future and to provide compulsory education to children.
j. What is common among most legal systems regarding property?
In most legal systems, a departed family member's property is dealt with by the deceased family member.

Critical Thinking

a. What changes have started to occur in Nepali families in recent days? What impacts will they bring on the society? Discuss.

In the 21st century, Nepali families are gradually shifting from traditional to modern cultural norms. People are increasingly prioritizing personal happiness and are opting for nuclear families. While this offers greater happiness and individual satisfaction, it has also led to a rapid increase in divorce rates due to misunderstandings. Both men and women are now sharing household roles and have equal rights within the family. This has led to a greater understanding among family members who can share their ideas and feelings freely.

There is also a decline in early, arranged marriages, with more individuals choosing their own partners. This suggests a shift in marital decision-making processes, where personal choice is becoming more important than family agreements. These changes will bring both positive and negative impacts on society. While they promote equality and individual happiness, they may also strain traditional family bonds and values.


b. We see many elderly people in the elderly homes these days in Nepal. Some of them are abandoned while others live there willingly. Do you think Nepali people are deviating from their traditional culture? Give reasons.

The increasing number of elderly people in nursing homes in Nepal indicates a deviation from traditional culture. Historically, the joint family system was dominant, with families sharing the responsibility of caring for their elders, providing emotional, psychological, and economic support, and giving them respect. This system was the socio-economic backbone of Nepalese society.

However, with modernization and new lifestyles, the traditional joint family is breaking down into nuclear families. This shift in family structure and values often compels the elderly to live alone or move to institutions. Reasons for this include the misbehavior of children, financial crises, a feeling of ignorance, and a lack of emotional support. Some elderly people willingly choose to live in these homes, viewing them as a better alternative for a problem-free life.

The traditional extended family had its advantages, such as a strong sense of community, cultural protection, and shared responsibilities, but also disadvantages like less privacy and potential for suppressed personality development. The modern nuclear family offers greater understanding, cooperation, and freedom for its members but can lead to a lack of security, proper care for the young and old, and a disconnect from traditional norms. While both family structures have their pros and cons, the rise of elderly homes clearly shows that Nepalese society is moving away from its traditional, integrated family system.
