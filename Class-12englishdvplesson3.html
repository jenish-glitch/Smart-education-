A. Find out the origin and the meaning of the following words from the text.

Penalty: From Middle French pénalité. Meaning: a punishment imposed for breaking a law, rule, or contract.

Major: From Latin word "great" and French word "majeur". Meaning: important, serious, or significant.

Stadium: From Latin word meaning "study," "zeal," "dedication". Meaning: A center for the study of the Liberal Arts.

Trophy: From the French trophée in 1513, Old French trophee, from Latin trophaeum. Meaning: A cup or other decorative object awarded as a prize for a victory or success.

Defender: From the Latin word defensor and Old French defendeor. Meaning: One who protects from injury, a champion.

Anthem: From Greek antiphōnos. Meaning: A rousing or uplifting song identified with a particular group, body, or cause.

Dejection: From the Latin deicere meaning "throw down." Meaning: A sad and depressed state; low spirits.

Jubilant: From Latin word jubilare. Meaning: Making a joyful noise.

Record: From Latin words cor and re-. Meaning: A thing constituting a piece of evidence about the past, especially an account kept in writing or some other permanent form.

Suave: From Latin word suavis means "agreeable." Meaning: Charming, confident, and elegant.

Podium: From Greek words pous and pod meaning "foot." Meaning: finish first, second, or third, so as to appear on a podium to receive an award.

Tournament: From old French torneiement. Meaning: A series of contests between a number of competitors, competing for an overall prize.

Reminiscent: From Latin word reminisce meaning remembering. Meaning: Tending to remind one of something.

Incredible: From Latin word In- meaning "not" and credibills meaning "believe."

Savor: From Latin word sapere meaning "to taste."

B. Consult a dictionary and define the following terms related to cricket.

  • Stump: Each of the three upright pieces of wood which form a wicket.
  • Crease: Any of a number of lines marked on the pitch at specified places.
  • Boundary: A hit crossing the limits of the field, scoring four or six runs.
  • Sixer: A hit for 6 runs.
  • Googly: Type of deceptive delivery bowled by a right-arm leg-spin bowler.
  • Leg-by: A run scored from a ball that has touched part of the batsman's body (apart from the hand) without touching the bat.
  • Wicket: Each of the sets of three stumps with two bails across the top at either end of the pitch.
  • Maiden: An over in which no runs are scored.
  • Pitch: The strip of ground between the two sets of stumps.
  • Power-play: The set of overs with special fielding rules during a limited-overs cricket match.
  • No-ball: An unlawfully delivered ball, counting one as an extra to the batting side if not otherwise scored from.
  • Yorker: A ball bowled that hits the cricket pitch around the batsman's feet.

Critical Thinking

a. "Every match is a new opportunity. Put its failure behind and start over again." Does this apply to the Italian football team when we analyse their performance from their failure to qualify for the World Cup Football to their victory in the European Championship in these four years?

Yes, the statement "Every match is a new opportunity. Put its failure behind and start over again" perfectly applies to the Italian football team. Following their failure to qualify for the World Cup for the first time in 60 years, the Italian team, under Roberto Mancini, underwent a radical transformation. They didn't dwell on their failure but used it as a catalyst for change and improvement. Mancini instilled a new culture of confidence, strategy, and organization. This enabled them to go on a national record of 34 games unbeaten and ultimately win the Euro 2020. Their journey is a testament to the power of learning from past failures and approaching every new match as a fresh opportunity to succeed.


b. When a team plays in a home ground, it gets a huge support from the audience in the stadium. Does this support them to win the match or the players may feel pressure to win and thus lose? What do you think?

Playing on home ground can have a dual effect on a team. On one hand, the massive support from home fans can provide a significant psychological and confidence boost, often referred to as the "home ground advantage." This can lead to better performance, as players feel more encouraged and motivated. The familiarity with the playing conditions, weather, and pitch also gives the home team a tactical edge.

However, this support can also create immense pressure. The expectation to perform well and not disappoint the home crowd can be overwhelming for some players. In the case of the Euro Cup 2020 final, England had not won a major tournament since 1966. This long wait created a burden of expectation, and the pressure from the home crowd at Wembley might have contributed to the team's lack of faith and eventual loss in the penalty shootout. Therefore, while home ground support can be a powerful tool for victory, it can also lead to increased pressure, which may cause players to falter and lose a match.
