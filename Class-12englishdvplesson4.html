Comprehension

Answer the following questions.

a. What is a hyperloop? How does it work?
A hyperloop is a planned train capable of traveling at incredible speeds. It works by being enclosed in a tube with no air, which eliminates friction and allows the train to travel much faster.
b. How is hyperloop more beneficial than the traditional trains?
The hyperloop is more beneficial than traditional trains because it's less expensive and uses less energy. The track doesn't need to provide constant electricity to the pods, and with pods departing every 30 seconds, it operates more like a demand-based service.
c. Does hyperloop have a successful history? How?
No, the hyperloop does not have a successful history in its current form. The original concept was to use wagons and pneumatic tubes to transport mail and parcels, not passengers.
d. Write the contributions of Robert Goddard and Elon Musk for the development of hyperloop.
Robert Goddard, in the early 20th century, created the 'vactrain' idea, which is a clear prototype of the hyperloop. Elon Musk, the founder of Space X and Tesla, revived interest in the idea in August 2013 with his 'Hyperloop Alpha' paper, envisioning a "fifth mode" of transportation for ultra-fast intercity travel.
e. What relation does speed have with air resistance? Explain.
Speed is inversely related to air resistance. More air resistance decreases the speed of a moving body, and less air resistance increases it. The hyperloop is designed to travel through vacuum tubes to eliminate air resistance, which drastically increases its speed.
f. What are hyperloop capsules compared with? How are they similar?
Hyperloop capsules are compared with a Boeing 747 because they both accelerate at a similar rate.
g. How do the passengers feel while travelling via hyperloop? What will be done to make it luxurious?
Passengers on a hyperloop will feel approximately the same as riding a lift or a passenger aircraft. To make the journey luxurious, the cabin will display beautiful scenery, and each passenger will have access to private entertainment systems.
h. Why does the writer doubt about the success of hyperloop? What does the success depend on?
The writer doubts the success of hyperloop due to the enormous multibillion-dollar expenditure and the fact that the projects are still in the pilot and experimental phases. Its success will depend on factors like destinations, the local economy, and the geographical location.

Critical Thinking

a. Is the hyperloop the future of transportation or just a dream? What do you think? Justify your opinion with suitable reasons.

The hyperloop, a conceptual transport system for passengers in pods within vacuum tubes, has the potential to be the future of transportation, not just a dream. While the concept of pneumatic transport isn't new, the application to human travel is a modern ambition. Just as early dreams of flight seemed impossible but are now commonplace, the hyperloop could become a reality.

If successful, it promises immense benefits: supersonic speeds, environmental friendliness, and potential economic success. It could drastically change how we travel, making long distances feel short. However, despite this promise, new technologies require significant time and investment to mature. The success will depend on overcoming massive engineering and financial challenges. Until then, it remains an ambitious but hopeful vision for the future.


b. The number of private vehicles is increasing day by day in Nepal beyond the capacity of our infrastructure. What do you think should be done to curb the ever-growing number of private vehicles? Discuss.

The increasing number of private vehicles in Nepal, outstripping the capacity of our infrastructure, poses a significant problem. To curb this trend, a multi-pronged approach is necessary. First, we must discourage private vehicle ownership. This can be done by implementing a system of progressive taxation on cars, where the tax rate increases with each additional vehicle a family owns, and by raising toll fares.

These measures, however, will only be effective if accompanied by a dramatic improvement in public transportation. The public transit system needs to be revamped to be more reliable, comfortable, and efficient. This includes running more buses during peak hours, ensuring they are punctual, and upgrading them with modern amenities like comfortable seating and air conditioning. Cleanliness and hygiene at bus and train stations must also be maintained to make public transport a dignified and pleasant experience. By making public transport a genuinely attractive alternative, we can reduce dependency on private vehicles and alleviate environmental and infrastructure pressures.
