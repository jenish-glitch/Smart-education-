Critical Thinking

B. Consult your teacher and define the following thinking skills.

a. Convergent thinking
Convergent thinking is a type of thinking that focuses on finding the single, well-established answer to a problem. It aims to derive the single best, or most correct, answer to a question.
b. Divergent thinking
Divergent thinking is a thought process or method used to generate creative ideas by exploring many possible solutions. Many solutions are explored in a short amount of time, and unexpected connections are often drawn.
c. Critical thinking
Critical thinking is the analysis of facts to form a judgment. It is a complex subject with several definitions, but it generally involves the rational, skeptical, unbiased analysis and evaluation of factual evidence.
d. Creative thinking
Creative thinking is the ability to generate a completely new, appealing, and useful thought or idea. It is a skill that enables you to come up with original and unconventional ideas.
